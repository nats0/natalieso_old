Check Up: An Edgar Wright inspired film

Recognized as an auteur, Edgar Wright is best known for showcasing humor within his fast-paced and kinetically edited sequences in his satirical films——and I wanted to emulate this in my own film.

Process:

    After watching—and loving— Edgar Wright’s Baby Driver (2017) and Scott Pilgrim vs the World (2010), I wanted to try my hand at creating a film inspired by the auteur’s unique style of kinetically edited sequences. Not only were did they need to be humorously fast-paced, yet have spaces of gradual pacing to contrast, but Wright also compliments them with a whimsical soundtrack. As all the sound effects had to be meticulously aligned with the clips, I chose to pair this stylistic editing with a theme surrounding what OCD ‘looks like’. Since most people many associate OCD with being overly organized, I wanted to challenge this overly simplified—and frankly, beautified—version of the disorder by having a plot twist to reveal that the character didn’t have OCD.

Apps Used:

  • Adobe After Effects
  • Final Cut Pro
